Tax Bracket Data Visualizer

What do tax brackets actually look like?

During a discussion with friends about income taxes in society, I realized that I had never really thought about tax brackets from a graphical point of view!

Thus, I did a little bit of research and decided to implement a responsive Python app that can graph the US tax brackets based on user input / any new tax rules. The following graphs show all four categories of income taxes at all their brackets, up to $250k. (Python code can graph up to an end income range of the user's choice.) These graphs confirm and/or provide some takeaways about taxes:

  • Getting married provides a large, non-trivial tax reduction
  • Similarly, filing as the head of household provides a non-trivial tax cut
  • Single filers and married separate filers are the same
  • Contrary to popular belief (and politicized arguments), your effective tax rate is much lower than your marginal tax rate
  • The biggest marginal increase in taxes paid is between the 22% and 24% brackets, which around the middle to upper-middle class
