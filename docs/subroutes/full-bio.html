About Me

Full Bio 📘

I started my journey in data as an undergraduate at the University of Pennsylvnia, intending to major in Economics and Statistics. Enjoyably, these subjects utilized my pattern-recognition and problem-solving skills as a means to generate tangible impact, and thus, I spent the larger part of my underclassman years gearing for a career in business intelligence and analytics.

However, around 3/5ths of the way through my undergraduate career, I discovered the exciting world of the art auction houses and the art market. Much the same in traditional business analytics, art speciality had to analytically uncover and iterate upon patterns to make impactful business decisions--only, their patterns drew upon a far greater range data: from sales histories to academic texts to visual art to even client preconceptions. Without the proper toolkit to effectively find those patterns in art, I became obsessive with connecting analytical theories to numerical patterns. This desire was the impetus for why I pivoted sharply into the areas I ultimately matriculated in: Philosophy, Art History, and Marketing.

Now a post-grad who has had his fill of the art market, I wish return to my original passions in data science and analysis. Hence, I immediately joined Penn's Data Science program after my Bachelors as a means to directly pursue my data science dreams. I believe my "detour" into philosophy and art speciality provides me with a unique and expanded problem-solving toolkit, as I can comfortably navigate a higher range of problems and data types, adapt myself to a larger pool of problem topics, and draw upon a greater breadth of diverse industry experience!
