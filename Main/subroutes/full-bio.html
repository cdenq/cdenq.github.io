Full Bio

My name is Christopher Denq, and I am current student in the University of Pennsylvania's Data Science graduate certification program.

FAQ

How did an undergrad in Philosophy and Art History turn into grad work in Data Science?

For three-fifths of my undergraduate career, I was pursuing a dual-degree from Penn's Wharton and CAS schools, focusing on economics and statistics. My ambition was aimed at business intellgience, and I was lucky enough to land a summer internship at McKinsey & Company as a business analyst intern.

Unfortunately around this time, I had a family situation back home, which combined with the general burnout of a being a junior-rising, prompted me to take one year off from school. During this self-care process, I discovered a lot of the same pattern-recognition processes in analytical philosophy and art history (out of all places!), as well as, many new pattern-recognition problems that I did not have the toolkit to solve. This drive to uncover numerical patterns (in the form of price evaluations) in an inherently non-numerical field (visual art) made me obssessive, which is why I pivoted so hard into art speciality when I returned to Penn. I finished two majors (Philosophy, Art History) and one minor (Consumer Psychology / Marketing) within two years, making my undergraduate career a total of five years (with one year break).

Having achieved the goals I wanted to in art speciality--which culminated in an internship at Christie's--I decided to pivot back to my original goals in data and analytics. Thus, upon graduating with my Bachelor's, I immediately went back to Penn to pursue my data science dreams as a post-grad!
